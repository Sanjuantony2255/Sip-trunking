What is SIP Trunking?

Session Initiation Protocol (SIP) Trunking is the digital method of making and receiving phone calls and other digital communication over an internet connection. It replaces traditional analog phone lines (PRI/ISDN).

Think of it as a virtual pipe that connects your on-premise Phone System (PBX) to the Public Switched Telephone Network (PSTN) via the Internet.

Scalable

Add channels instantly without physical wiring.

Reliable

Geo-redundancy and failover capabilities.

The 4 Core VoIP Protocols

Modern VoIP isn't just one thing. It's a symphony of four distinct protocols working together.

SIP

The Traffic Controller

Handles call setup, control, and termination. It does NOT carry audio.

SDP

The Negotiator

Defines the "rules" for media: codecs, IP addresses, and ports.

RTP

The Delivery Truck

Streams the actual voice and video data in real-time.

RTCP

The Quality Inspector

Monitors call quality (jitter, packet loss) and reports back.

Interactive Call Flow

Click through the steps to understand exactly what happens when you make a call.

1. INVITE

Caller sends a request to initiate a session. Contains SDP offer.

2. 100 Trying / 180 Ringing

Server acknowledges receipt and indicates the callee's phone is ringing.

3. 200 OK

Callee answers. Contains SDP answer (agreed codecs/ports).

4. ACK & RTP Stream

Caller acknowledges connection. Audio (RTP) begins flowing directly between devices.

5. BYE

Either party hangs up. Session is terminated.
